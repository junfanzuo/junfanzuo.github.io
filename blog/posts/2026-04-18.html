1. a-Stems

1.1. a-Stem Nouns

An a-stem noun is a noun whose base uninflected form (its stem) ends in the short vowel a. They are always either masculine (MASC) or neuter (NTR). NTR a-stem nouns share the exact same endings as MASC a-stem nouns for most cases. They only differ in the NOM, VOC, and ACC cases. Furthermore, within any given number (SG, DU, or PL), a NTR noun will always have identical forms for its NOM and ACC cases. Consider the following tables for a MASC a-stem noun (बाल bāla) and a NTR a-stem noun (फल phala), respectively:

बाल bāla (MASC)
Singular Dual Plural
Nominative (Nom) बालः bālaḥ बालौ bālau बालाः bālāḥ
Vocative (Voc) बाल bāla बालाः bālāḥ
Accusative (Acc) बालम् bālam बालान् bālān
Instrumental (Ins) बालेन bālena बालाभ्याम् bālābhyām बालैः bālaiḥ
Dative (Dat) बालाय bālāya बालेभ्यः bālebhyaḥ
Ablative (Abl) बालात् bālāt
Genitive (Gen) बालस्य bālasya बालयोः bālayoḥ बालानाम् bālānām
Locative (Loc) बाले bāle बालेषु bāleṣu

फल phala (NTR)
Singular Dual Plural
Nominative (Nom) फलम् phalam फले phale फलानि phalāni
Vocative (Voc) फल phala
Accusative (Acc) फलम् phalam
Instrumental (Ins) फलेन phalena फलाभ्याम् phalābhyām फलैः phalaiḥ
Dative (Dat) फलाय phalāya फलेभ्यः phalebhyaḥ
Ablative (Abl) फलात् phalāt
Genitive (Gen) फलस्य phalasya फलयोः phalayoḥ फलानाम् phalānām
Locative (Loc) फले phale फलेषु phaleṣu

1.2. a-Stem Adjectives

For a-Stems, the MASC adjectives have the same forms as the corresponding MASC nouns and the NTR adjectives have the same forms as the corresponding NTR nouns. FEM a-Stem adjectives follow the forms of ā-Stem nouns, however. Consider the following tables for the forms of an a-Stem adjective (नव nava):

MASC नव nava
Singular Dual Plural
Nominative (Nom) नवः navaḥ नवौ navau नवाः navāḥ
Vocative (Voc) नव nava
Accusative (Acc) नवम् navam नवान् navān
Instrumental (Ins) नवेन navena नवाभ्याम् navābhyām नवैः navaiḥ
Dative (Dat) नवाय navāya नवेभ्यः navebhyaḥ
Ablative (Abl) नवात् navāt
Genitive (Gen) नवस्य navasya नवयोः navayoḥ नवानाम् navānām
Locative (Loc) नवे nave नवेषु naveṣu
NTR नव nava
Singular Dual Plural
Nominative (Nom) नवम् navam नवे nave नवानि navāni
Vocative (Voc) नव nava
Accusative (Acc) नवम् navam
Instrumental (Ins) नवेन navena नवाभ्याम् navābhyām नवैः navaiḥ
Dative (Dat) नवाय navāya नवेभ्यः navebhyaḥ
Ablative (Abl) नवात् navāt
Genitive (Gen) नवस्य navasya नवयोः navayoḥ नवानाम् navānām
Locative (Loc) नवे nave नवेषु naveṣu

FEM नव nava*
Singular Dual Plural
Nominative (Nom) नवा navā नवे nave नवाः navāḥ
Vocative (Voc) नवे nave
Accusative (Acc) नवाम् navām
Instrumental (Ins) नवया navayā नवाभ्याम् navābhyām नवाभिः navābhiḥ
Dative (Dat) नवायै navāyai नवाभ्यः navābhyaḥ
Ablative (Abl) नवायाः navāyāḥ
Genitive (Gen) नवयोः navayoḥ नवानाम् navānām
Locative (Loc) नवायाम् navāyām नवासु navāsu

*Since we need MASC adjectives to describe MASC nouns, and NTR adjectives to describe NTR nouns, one problem pops up: Why do we need FEM a-Stem adjectives if a-Stem nouns are exclusively MASC or NTR (i.e., FEM a-Stem nouns do not exist)? Answer: FEM a-Stem adjectives do not exist. When an adjective needs to describe a FEM noun, the adjective changes its stem by transforming into either ā-Stem or ī-Stem.

2. ā-Stems

2.1. ā-Stem Nouns

ā-Stem nouns are FEM only**. Consider the following table for the forms of an ā-Stem noun (कथा kathā):

कथा kathā (FEM)
Singular Dual Plural
Nominative (Nom) कथा kathā कथे kathe कथाः kathāḥ
Vocative (Voc) कथे kathe
Accusative (Acc) कथाम् kathām
Instrumental (Ins) कथया kathayā कथाभ्याम् kathābhyām कथाभिः kathābhiḥ
Dative (Dat) कथायै kathāyai कथाभ्यः kathābhyaḥ
Ablative (Abl) कथायाः kathāyāḥ
Genitive (Gen) कथयोः kathayoḥ कथानाम् kathānām
Locative (Loc) कथायाम् kathāyām कथासु kathāsu

**Or I should say, 99% of ā-Stem nouns are FEM except some exceptions like विश्वपा viśvapā (MASC).

2.2. ā-Stem Adjectives

We have the same problem here: ā-Stem nouns are FEM only, and what should we do if we want to describe a MASC or NTR noun? There is no such thing as a MASC or NTR ā-Stem adjective because ā- is a FEM marker. Instead of forming MASC or NTR words out of ā-Stems, we only create an ā-Stem when your a-Stem adjective is forced to describe a FEM noun. Consider the following tables for the adjective शुभ śubha, and notice how the MASC and NTR tables used the a-Stem while the FEM table uses the transformed ā-Stem:

MASC शुभ śubha
Singular Dual Plural
Nominative (Nom) शुभः śubhaḥ शुभौ śubhau शुभाः śubhāḥ
Vocative (Voc) शुभ śubha
Accusative (Acc) शुभम् śubham शुभान् śubhān
Instrumental (Ins) शुभेन śubhena शुभाभ्याम् śubhābhyām शुभैः śubhaiḥ
Dative (Dat) शुभाय śubhāya शुभेभ्यः śubhebhyaḥ
Ablative (Abl) शुभात् śubhāt
Genitive (Gen) शुभस्य śubhasya शुभयोः śubhayoḥ शुभानाम् śubhānām
Locative (Loc) शुभे śubhe शुभेषु śubheṣu

NTR शुभ śubha
Singular Dual Plural
Nominative (Nom) शुभम् śubham शुभे śubhe शुभानि śubhāni
Vocative (Voc) शुभ śubha
Accusative (Acc) शुभम् śubham
Instrumental (Ins) शुभेन śubhena शुभाभ्याम् śubhābhyām शुभैः śubhaiḥ
Dative (Dat) शुभाय śubhāya शुभेभ्यः śubhebhyaḥ
Ablative (Abl) शुभात् śubhāt
Genitive (Gen) शुभस्य śubhasya शुभयोः śubhayoḥ शुभानाम् śubhānām
Locative (Loc) शुभे śubhe शुभेषु śubheṣu

FEM शुभ śubha
Singular Dual Plural
Nominative (Nom) शुभा śubhā शुभे śubhe शुभाः śubhāḥ
