[MISC] On Fragrances

26 Mar 2026

A. Some Thoughts

Fragrances are a very interesting topic. I have been collecting fragrances for a long time and I find that they can be very personal and subjective. This post is about fragrances I used previously. It might be helpful if you are going to buy one from the following list. But just as I said, what works for me might not work for you.

B. The Review

  • Dark Amber & Ginger Lily, Cologne Intense, Jo Malone

This was my very first fragrance. It is woody in a comfortable way. It is low-key, restrained but classic with a bit sweetness. The best in Jo Malone.

  • English Oak & Hazelnut, Cologne, Jo Malone

I tried this at a Jo Malone store in HK and I fell in love with it instantly. I bought it immediately but after wearing it for a few days, I realized it wasn't for me. English Oak & Hazelnut is too strong and sharp when you spray it. You can actually feel the alcohol. Before I gave it to my girlfriend, I used it as a room deodorizer.

  • Sauvage, EDP, Dior

Sauvage reminds me of shampoo and body wash that you can buy at stores. It is just too regular and plain. Won't buy it again. Another thing is that Dior's face cleanser and shaving gel contain Sauvage as well. It makes me sick to smell Sauvage when everything is Sauvage.

  • Ébène Fumé, EDP, Tom Ford

This is the GOAT. I have nothing to say about it. I wish that one day Tom Ford would release a Parfum/Extrait version of Ébène Fumé.

  • White Suede, EDP, Tom Ford

Very, very fine fragrance. It's so special, and it reminds me of Kal'tsit's knitwear by the fireplace. I don't have any further comments and it is in the GOAT tier.

  • Café Rose, EDP, Tom Ford

Nice rose fragrance. But that's all - it is just a regular, plain rose fragrance. I think all rose fragrances can do the same. I was deciding between this and Ombré Leather and I bought this one. I am not a fan of rose, however. But I bought it at a very low price at Schiphol Airport so I feel okay even if I don't wear it.

  • Neroli Portofino, EDP, Tom Ford

Meh-tier Tom Ford frangance. Fine for summer, but too regular: citrus, oranges, lime... I think all the summer frangances are the same and I'm not particularly into summer frangances. That means you can buy Eau d'Orange Verte or any other affordable summer fragrance for a much lower price and they will do the same. Would buy it again for my summer wear if Tom Ford offers it at 50% off.

  • Vanille Fatale, EDP, Tom Ford

Strong alcohol scent when sprayed, and then a combination of vanilla ice cream and strawberry bubblegum comes out. It's fruity, but too sweet for me. I would say it's meh-tier in Tom Ford.

  • Gentle Fluidity Silver, EDP, Maison Francis Kurkdjian

Beautiful, beautiful, beautiful fragrance. Very floral, sometimes sweet. I'm not a fan of floral fragrances, but this one is quite pleasant because it's not too overpowering. Would buy it again and again.

  • Baccarat Rouge 540, EDP, Maison Francis Kurkdjian

This one has very positive reviews online - I don't know why. This is the second time I've used 'strange' to describe a fragrance. My first 'strange' fragrance is Fucking Fabulous EDP - I tried it at a Tom Ford shop and it is super strange and weird (and then I bought White Suede). I don't know what the smell is. For Baccarat Rouge 540, I smell the sweetness only. But I'm not sure what kind of sweetness is that. This one is very pricy so think twice before buying.

  • Eau d'Orange Verte, EDC, Hermès

Classic summer fragrance. The orange scent is comfortable, refreshing but won't be too strong. I think you can buy 3-5 bottles of Eau d'Orange Verte with the money buying one bottle of Neroli Portofino. Summer fragrances are the same anyway - or perhaps there is something wrong with my perception.

  • Un Jardin Sur Le Nil, EDT, Hermès

Regular orange. Not impressed. This is the one that they will use for room fragrance in those boutiques.

  • Bleu de Chanel, EDP, Chanel

This is Chanel's version of Sauvage. Too plain. What I mean by 'plain' is that it lacks character and uniqueness that allow you to recognize it. You don't have the "Oh, this is the xxx" feeling when you try Bleu or Sauvage. I won't buy any fragrance from Dior or Chanel.

  • Above Us, Steorra, EDP, Aesop

Aesop is a very unique brand for both skincare and fragrance. I have yet to find an Aesop product that doesn't smell good. For Above Us, Steorra, I smell the incense in the temple and a little spiciness from pepper (the pepper is a bit similar to the smell of sweat though, but it will fade away soon). Overall, it's a pleasant fragrance. Removing the spiciness would make it even better, in my opinion.

C. Notes

1. A Note on Longevity: Out of all the fragrances I've used so far, I have yet to find one with truly impressive longevity. The fragrances on the list above generally fade away within 1 to 3 hours.

2. Perfect Lily: I've always been searching for a pure lily fragrance. Since lilies don't yield essential oils, true lily fragrances are actually quite rare on the market. My very first lily scent was Cartier's Baiser Volé. It has an incredibly clean lily note and is also affordable. As for Dark Amber & Ginger Lily, the lily scent in it can be quite faint. I'm planning to try Serge Lutens' Un Lys and Byredo's Casablanca Lily next, and I'll update this post once I do.
